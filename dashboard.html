Hibern8 Ctrl+Space — Help & About

Reclaim browser memory with user-controlled tab hibernation. This panel is your quick reference.

🔒 No information leaves your device through Hibern8 Ctrl+Space. It has no servers and sends nothing about you, your tabs, or your browsing anywhere. Everything runs locally in your browser profile.

What it does

Hibern8 Ctrl+Space uses the browser's own native tab-discard to unload idle tabs and return their memory to your system, while keeping you in control. Hibernated tabs reload from source when you return to them — the design relies on cheap, fast bandwidth instead of hoarding tab data in local memory.

The controls

  • 💤 Hibernate all except audio/video — sweeps every window, sparing tabs playing sound, plus pinned and active tabs.
  • 🔇 Mute all — silences every sounding tab across all windows.
  • Auto-unmute active — after Mute all, the tab you switch to (or that starts new audio) unmutes itself; the background stays muted. Tabs you muted by hand are left alone.
  • 🪟 Windows — hibernate or explore any window, even background ones. Each window's "Explore tabs" lists the active tab first, shaded by state (active / awake / hibernated).
  • 🔊 Audio / Video Tabs — every sounding tab, pinned to the top, with Go and Mute.
  • Sort / Appearance — alphabetize the lists, and choose Auto / Light / Dark.
  • ↻ Refresh — re-reads tab state with a brief visual cue.
  • ⌨ Ctrl+Space — open Hibern8 Ctrl+Space from anywhere. Change or reassign the shortcut at chrome://extensions/shortcuts (Chrome/Edge).

Prove the memory savings

Open your browser's task manager — Shift+Esc in Chrome/Edge, or about:performance in Firefox — and watch the memory of hibernated tabs collapse. The in-app number is an estimate; the task manager is the truth.

Private / incognito tabs

Browsers hide private windows from every extension by default. To let Mute all / Hibernate all and the private audio-video section reach them, enable Allow in Incognito (Chrome), Allow in InPrivate (Edge), or Run in Private Windows (Firefox) in the extension's details. Private tabs are never listed in the normal sections.

Design principles

Hibern8 Ctrl+Space is built on a few simple ideas: you stay in control (nothing is hibernated against your wishes — active, pinned, and audio/video tabs are always spared); least-privilege and local-only (it asks for the minimum access, keeps everything in your browser profile, and sends nothing anywhere); opt-in for anything sensitive (the screen-time timer and Safe-Hold are off until you turn them on); no page access (Hibern8 Ctrl+Space never reads or edits the content of your web pages); lean on cheap bandwidth, not hoarded memory (a hibernated tab reloads from its own source on wake rather than holding RAM); and honest estimates (the in-app memory and power figures are illustrative — your browser's task manager shows the true numbers).

Related research

Independent benchmarks and browser-vendor analyses consistently find that hibernating idle tabs cuts memory, background CPU, and — by extension — power, most noticeably on laptop battery life. Hibern8 Ctrl+Space layers user-directed control on top of Chrome's native tab-discard:

  • Memory: Chrome's built-in Memory Saver discards inactive tabs for roughly a 30–40% memory reduction in typical multi-tab use; 30–50+ open tabs can otherwise hold 2–6 GB of RAM, most of which hibernation returns to the OS.
  • CPU & battery: lower sustained CPU (on the order of ~29% in some 2026 benchmarks) yields measurable battery extension — roughly 36–48 minutes over multi-hour sessions.
  • Per tab: proactive, timer-based suspension can reclaim 90–95% of an individual tab's working set before the system is under memory pressure — more than reactive, wait-for-low-memory approaches.
  • Native vs. extension: native Memory Saver is automatic but reactive and opaque; user-controlled tools add proactive, window-level, audio-aware bulk actions and transparent metrics. Hibern8 Ctrl+Space uses the same native discard API while closing those gaps, and points you to the task manager for ground-truth numbers.

The benefit also ripples outward: you save battery, heat, and electricity; the sites you leave open stop being polled by idle tabs, easing load on their servers; and network & data-center infrastructure carries less always-on background chatter.

How the estimate is calculated (worked example)

Every figure here comes from three inputs you can check yourself — nothing is hidden:

  • Per idle tab ≈ 0.5 W saved — a representative continuous background draw (timers, ads, analytics, scripts) that stops when the tab is discarded.
  • Active use ≈ 6 hours/day — the machine isn't on 24/7, so savings only count while you're using it.
  • The formula: watts saved = idle tabs × 0.5 W, then kWh/year = watts × 6 h/day × 365 ÷ 1000.

Worked out for a few everyday tab counts (these are the idle tabs Hibern8 Ctrl+Space hibernates — your active tab and any media tab stay awake):

Reading the 10-tab row step by step: 10 × 0.5 W = 5 W; 5 W × 6 h = 30 Wh/day; 30 Wh × 365 = ~11 kWh/year; at 12 Wh per phone charge that's ~900 charges, and at ~0.30 kWh per mile, ~37 EV miles.

Plus media use: a playing video or music tab is always spared (never hibernated), and it alone typically draws ~2–4 W while decoding — often more than several idle tabs combined. Hibern8 Ctrl+Space keeps that tab smooth and frees the idle tabs around it. So while a movie plays in one window, hibernating your other 5–15 idle tabs still delivers the savings above; and when you're finished watching, closing or hibernating that media tab is the single biggest cut of all.

Public-network privacy — Safe-Hold

🛡 Suspend session holds all of the browser's outbound page traffic, pauses any in-progress downloads (and new ones started while held, resuming them on release), and keeps tabs from loading until you release it — so nothing quietly reloads or keeps transferring on airport or café Wi‑Fi before your VPN is up. Release is always manual. In ⚙ Settings you can choose when Hibern8 Ctrl+Space opens (at startup and/or after the previous session is restored), have it prompt on session restore ("are these tabs safe to load, or do you need a VPN first?"), hold previous-session tabs until you manually refresh them, engage Safe-Hold automatically at startup, and require you to confirm your VPN is connected before releasing. (A browser extension can't actually detect a VPN, so this is a manual confirmation — releasing asks you to confirm your VPN is up.) The honest caveat: the strongest protection is your VPN client's own kill-switch, which blocks every app system-wide — Safe-Hold is a browser-level convenience layer on top of that, not a replacement.

Development & security

Hibern8 Ctrl+Space is built least-privilege and local-only. Its pages run under a strict Content-Security-Policy (default-src 'self'; script-src 'self'; object-src 'none'; connect-src 'self'; base-uri 'none'; frame-ancestors 'none'; form-action 'none') that blocks any inline or remotely-hosted script from executing; there is no remote code or eval, no third-party network calls, and all dynamic text is HTML-escaped to prevent script or HTML injection. Any URL Hibern8 Ctrl+Space navigates to is validated to http/https only. An injection watchdog also listens for CSP violations: if a script injection is ever blocked, Hibern8 Ctrl+Space warns you in-panel, names the source, and raises a notification urging an antivirus scan (extensions can't signal antivirus directly, so this alert is how a scan is prompted). Hibern8 Ctrl+Space requests no host permissions and never reads or edits the content of your web pages. Your preferences and saved data stay in your browser profile on this device (in local extension storage) — Hibern8 Ctrl+Space has no servers and transmits nothing about you. Two honest clarifications: favicons come from your browser's own local cache (Chrome/Edge) or the site's icon URL (Firefox), and waking a hibernated tab reloads that page from its own source over the network — that's the tab's normal traffic, not Hibern8 Ctrl+Space sending your data anywhere.
