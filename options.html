Privacy & Safe-Hold (public-network protection)

Safe-Hold keeps hibernated and restored tabs from loading — and holds the browser's outbound traffic — until you release it, so tabs don't quietly reload on untrusted Wi‑Fi before your VPN is up. Release is always manual. Note: the strongest protection is your VPN client's own kill-switch; this is a browser-level convenience layer on top of it.

A browser extension can't actually detect a VPN, so this is a manual confirmation: when enabled, releasing the hold asks you to confirm your VPN is connected.
